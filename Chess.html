Chess board game

Chess:Overview, Rules and Regulations

What is Chess?

Chess is a board game that is played on a board with 64 squares(8 squares on each side of the board).

The game is played by two players with each player having a set of pieces that are color coded either black or white. The player with the white pieces will usually play first according to the rule book of the International Chess Federation

How is Chess played?

In chess, the players usually have pieces with different movements and characteristics

The characteristics and names of these pieces are as follows:

  1. Pawns

    2. The pawns are pieces that move forward only. During the first move of the pawns, they can either move one or two squares forward but after that they can only move one square forward. The pawns can only capture pieces that are on the squares that are on the immediate diagonals as the pawn itself. There is a row of pawns on the 2nd row(the side closest to the player). When the pawn reaches the 8th row of the board(The row furthest from the player), the pawn is promoted to any of the pieces that will be discussed in the later sections.This piece is worth one point.

    Pawn movement

  2. Rooks

    3. The rook is a piece that is able to move in straight paths(horizontally and vertically). It can move either forwards, bacwards, left and right.This piece is worth 5 points and is located on the extreme right and left of the board on the first row(also called rank).

    Rook Movement

  3. Knights

    4. A knight moves in a typical 'L' shape. Two squares vertically and then one square horizontally or vice versa. This piece is able to jump over the row of pawns that are infront of it. This piece is located beside the rooks and is worth 3 points. It captures in the manner that it moves. This piece cannot jump over the pawns.

    Knights Movement

  4. Bishops

    5. A bishop moves in a diagonal path in any direction across the board.This piece is located beside the knights and its values is 3 points. It captures in the direction of its movement. This piece cannot jump over the pawns.

    Bishop's Movement

  5. Queen

    6. The queen is one of the most powerful pieces in chess since it has a point worth of 8. This piece is a combination of boht the rook and the bishop. The queen moves horizontally, vertically and diagonally. For white, this piece is beside the bishop on the left while for black, this piece is beside the bishop on the right. This piece cannot jump over the pawns.

    Queen's Movement

  6. 6.King

    7. The king is the most important pieces of the chess game since he defines if a game is won ,lost or drawn. The king only moves one square around himself and is beside the queen in the only available space. The king cannot be captured unlike the other pieces.

    King's Movements
